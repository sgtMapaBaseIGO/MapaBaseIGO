Teselas vectoriales CARTO

(BORRADOR/CONTENIDO PROVISIONAL)


Las teselas vectoriales de CARTO están construidas a partir de la base de datos geográfica OpenStreetMap

carto.streets v1 schema

landcover


Id: landcover
Descripción: Landcover is used to describe the physical material at the surface of the earth. Land covers include grass, asphalt, trees, bare ground, water, etc.
Geometría: Polygon (MultiPolygon)
Zoom mínimo: 0
Zoom máximo: 14
Campos:

Campo Tipo (class) Valor Descripción (OpenStreetMap)
class string
farmland An area of farmland used for tillage and pasture (animals, crops, vegetables, flowers, fruit growing).
grass An area of mown and managed grass. Some view this as not a landuse, see the main page landuse=grass for information.
ice Ice.
rock A notable rock or group of rocks attached to the underlying bedrock.
sand An area covered by sand with no or very little vegetation.
wetland A natural area subject to inundation or with waterlogged ground.
wood Forest. Sometimes considered to have restricted meaning "Woodland with no forestry".
subclass string
(farmland) farmland An area of farmland used for tillage and pasture.
(farmland) orchard intentional planting of trees or shrubs maintained for food production.
(farmland) vineyard A piece of land where grapes are grown.
(grass) allotments A separate settlement, which is located outside an officially inhabited locality and has its own addressing.
(grass) garden A place where flowers and other plants are grown in a decorative and structured manner or for scientific purposes.
(grass) grass An area of mown and managed grass, e.g. a lawn in a park, the middle of a roundabout, part of a golf course, the side of the road etc. Maybe consider surface=grass or landcover=grass.
(grass) grassland Natural areas where the vegetation is dominated by grasses (Poaceae) and other herbaceous (non-woody) plants.
(grass) meadow Used to tag an area of land primarily vegetated by grass plus other non-woody plants, mainly used for hay (meadow) or for grazing animals (pasture).
(grass) park A park, usually municipal.
(grass) recreation_ground An open green space for general recreation, which often includes formal or informal pitches, nets and so on. Often municipally owned, sometimes part of colleges / companies or commercially operated.
(ice) glacier A permanent body of ice formed naturally from snow that is moving under its own weight.
(ice) ice_shelf Thick plates of ice (thickness of 100m and more).
(rock) bare_rock An area with sparse or no vegetation, so that the bedrock becomes visible.
(rock) scree Unconsolidated angular rocks formed by rockfall and weathering from adjacent rockfaces.
(sand) sand An area covered by sand with no or very little vegetation.
(wetland) wetland A natural area subject to inundation or with waterlogged ground.
(wood) forest Managed forest or woodland plantation (Other languages). Some use this to map an area of trees rather than the use of the land. See the OSM wiki page on Forest.
(wood) scrub Uncultivated land covered with bushes or stunted trees.
(wood) wood Forest. Sometimes considered to have restricted meaning "Woodland with no forestry".
_col Integer Coordenada "x" de la tesela para el nivel de zoom.
_row Integer Coordenada "y" de la tesela para el nivel de zoom.
_zoom Integer Nivel de zoom.


place


Id: place
Descripción: Used to indicate that a particular location is known by a particular name, to indicate what sort of "place" it is. A place tag should exist for every significant human settlements (city, town, suburb, etc.) and also for notable unpopulated, named places.
Geometría: Point (Point)
Zoom mínimo: 0
Zoom máximo: 14

4-8 district, county
6-8 municipality
6-9 city
7-10 town, village, hamlet
8-10 borough, suburb
8-11 quarter, neighbourhood, block		 The capital of a country or administrative divisions within countries.
class string
city The largest urban settlement or settlements within the territory.
continent One of the seven continents: Africa, Antarctica, Asia, Australia, Europe, North America, South America.
country A nation state or other high-level national political/administrative area.
hamlet A smaller rural community, typically with fewer than 100-200 inhabitants, and few infrastructure.
hamlet A smaller rural community, typically with fewer than 100-200 inhabitants, and few infrastructure.
island Any piece of land that is completely surrounded by water and isolated from other significant landmasses.
neighbourhood A neighbourhood is a smaller named, geographically localised place within a suburb of a larger city or within a town or village.
state A large sub-national political/administrative area.
suburb A part of a town or city with a well-known name and often a distinct identity.
town An important urban centre, between a village and a city in size.
village A smaller distinct settlement, smaller than a town with few facilities available with people traveling to nearby towns to access these.
iso_a2 string Two-letter country code ISO 3166-1 alpha-2.

river For narrow rivers which will be rendered as a line.
stream A naturally-formed waterway that is too narrow to be classed as a river. An active, able-bodied person should be able to jump over it if trees along it aren't too thick.
